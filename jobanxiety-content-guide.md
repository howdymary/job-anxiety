# JobAnxiety.ai — SEO Content Strategy Guide
### Version 1.0 · March 2026

---

## 1. Platform Positioning

**One-line**: JobAnxiety.ai is the data-driven destination for workers navigating AI-era career uncertainty — combining verified layoff tracking, curated AI job listings, and editorial career guidance in a single platform.

**SEO Positioning Statement**: JobAnxiety.ai should own the search territory between "I'm worried about losing my job to AI" and "I lost my job, now what?" No other platform bridges the emotional reality of career anxiety with the hard data and actionable resources needed to move forward.

**Content Mission**: Every piece of content on JobAnxiety.ai exists to do one of three things — validate the reader's concern with real data, equip them with a concrete next step, or connect them with an opportunity. Content that does none of these doesn't get published.

## 2. User Personas

### Persona A: "The Anxious Employee" (Pre-Displacement)

**Who they are**: Currently employed, 28–55, reads news about AI replacing jobs and feels a knot in their stomach. They Google things at 11pm. They haven't been laid off — but they're watching their industry change and wondering if they're next.

**Demographics & Psychographics**:
- Mid-career professionals in finance, legal, customer service, marketing, content, admin, and mid-level tech
- Household income $50K–$150K
- Many have families and financial obligations that make career disruption feel catastrophic
- Tend to catastrophize in private but project stability in public
- Information-seeking behavior: binge-research followed by paralysis
- Trust signals that matter to them: data sources, expert credentials, "people like me" stories

**Search Behavior**:
- Searches: "will AI replace [my job title]", "is [my industry] safe from AI", "AI job loss statistics 2026", "how to future-proof my career"
- Time of day: evenings and weekends (personal anxiety, not work research)
- Device: mobile-heavy (72%+ for anxiety-driven searches)
- Session pattern: multiple short visits, bookmarks content for later, shares with partner or close friend

**Content Needs**:
- Honest assessments of AI's impact on specific roles (not "everything will be fine" copium)
- Data-backed career transition guides
- Skills gap analysis: "what I know vs what the market wants"
- Stories from people who successfully pivoted
- The Displacement Ratio data (jobs created vs jobs lost) to give them proportion

**Conversion Path**:
```
Google search → lands on role-specific AI impact article
→ sees data dashboard embedded in article
→ clicks to explore trends for their industry
→ subscribes to weekly market brief ("I want to know before it happens")
→ returns weekly, eventually explores job listings
```

### Persona B: "The Recently Displaced" (Post-Layoff)

**Who they are**: Lost their job in the last 0–90 days. May or may not be related to AI. They're in a mix of shock, grief, and forced productivity. They need to figure out what's next while managing the emotional weight of being let go.

**Demographics & Psychographics**:
- Skews slightly younger (25–45) due to tech industry layoff patterns
- Disproportionately from tech, media, finance, and customer operations
- Experiencing identity disruption: "I was a [title] at [company], who am I now?"
- Oscillating between grief and manic job-searching
- Wary of toxic positivity ("this is a blessing in disguise!")
- Need practical help but also need to feel seen
- Trust signals: specificity, recency, real company names and numbers

**Search Behavior**:
- Searches: "laid off what to do now", "tech layoffs [month year]", "[company name] layoffs", "how to explain being laid off in interview", "career change at [age]", "jobs hiring now AI"
- Time of day: all day (they have time now; job searching is the new full-time job)
- Device: desktop-heavy (serious job searching happens on desktop)
- Session pattern: long sessions, deep engagement, high bookmark rate

**Content Needs**:
- Immediate practical guides: severance negotiation, COBRA, unemployment benefits by state
- Job board with real, verified, current listings (they're exhausted by ghosted applications)
- Career transition frameworks: skills-mapping, portfolio-building, interview prep
- Industry-specific guides: "I was in [X], here's what's hiring in adjacent fields"
- Emotional content: essays from people who've been through it (not advice — empathy)
- Social proof on job listings: "142 people applied today" (tells them this listing is active and real)

**Conversion Path**:
```
Google search → lands on "[Company] layoffs" news page or "laid off what to do" guide
→ sees layoff tracker with their company's event, feels validated
→ clicks to explore "people also displaced from [industry]" career guides
→ subscribes to job alerts for specific AI categories
→ applies directly through aggregated listings
→ returns daily during job search
```

### Persona C: "The Market Watcher" (Journalist / Researcher / Analyst)

**Who they are**: They're not personally anxious — they're tracking the AI labor market professionally. Journalists writing stories, policy analysts preparing briefs, HR leaders benchmarking, investors evaluating labor market exposure.

**Demographics & Psychographics**:
- Journalists at tech/business desks, policy researchers, HR/People Analytics professionals, VC analysts
- Value methodology, data provenance, and citation-ready formatting above all else

- Time of day: business hours
- Device: desktop
- Session pattern: deep research sessions, multiple data exports, API exploration

**Content Needs**:
- Downloadable datasets (CSV, JSON)
- Embeddable charts with attribution
- API access for custom queries
- Methodology documentation (versioned)
- Monthly/quarterly data briefs
- Press page with pre-formatted citations
- Corrections log (shows editorial integrity)

**Conversion Path**:
```
Google search → lands on trends dashboard or snapshot page
→ examines methodology page to assess credibility
→ downloads CSV or embeds chart in their article
```

## 3. Keyword Strategy

### Keyword Architecture

The keyword strategy follows a **hub-and-spoke model**: each content pillar (hub) targets a high-volume head term, and individual pages (spokes) target long-tail variations that link back to the hub. This tells Google you have topical authority across the entire domain.

```
HUB: "AI layoffs" (pillar page: /layoffs)
  ├── SPOKE: "AI layoffs 2026" (snapshot page)
  ├── SPOKE: "[Company] layoffs AI" (company pages)
  ├── SPOKE: "AI layoffs by industry" (dashboard filter view)
  ├── SPOKE: "WARN Act AI layoffs" (evergreen guide)
  └── SPOKE: "will AI cause more layoffs" (career note)

HUB: "AI jobs" (pillar page: /jobs)
  ├── SPOKE: "AI jobs remote" (filtered view)
  ├── SPOKE: "ML engineer jobs 2026" (category page)
  ├── SPOKE: "prompt engineer salary" (career guide)
```

## 4. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Layoff Intelligence

**Purpose**: Verified, source-attributed layoff tracking that Persona B needs for validation and Persona C needs for citation.

**Content Types**:

1. **Layoff Event Pages** (`/layoffs/[company-slug]`)
   - Auto-generated when a new layoff event is ingested
   - Structured: Company, date, affected count, confidence level, sources, WARN filings
   - Cross-referenced with the company's active job postings
   - Updated as new information emerges (corrections logged publicly)
   - Schema: Organization + NewsArticle JSON-LD

2. **Monthly Layoff Roundups** (`/career-notes/layoffs-[month]-[year]`)
   - Published on the 3rd business day of each month
   - Format: data summary (top 5 events, totals, AI-attribution rate) + Economist commentary + "what this means for job seekers" section
   - Word count: 1,200–1,800 words
   - Must include: at least one original chart from Data Visualist, downloadable CSV
   - Internal links to: relevant company pages, methodology, trends dashboard

3. **Industry Displacement Analysis** (`/career-notes/ai-impact-[industry]`)
   - Evergreen content, updated quarterly
   - Format: data-driven analysis of AI's impact on a specific industry (finance, legal, marketing, customer service, media, manufacturing, healthcare, education)
   - Word count: 2,500–4,000 words
   - Must include: displacement ratio for that industry, specific role-by-role breakdown, skills transition map, real job listings in adjacent roles
   - This is the primary SEO play for "will AI replace [role]" queries

**Publishing Cadence**:
- Layoff event pages: real-time (automated)
- Monthly roundups: 1st week of each month
- Industry analyses: quarterly refresh cycle (2–3 industries per quarter)

**Quality Standards (Journalist Agent enforced)**:
- Every claim has a linked source
- Confidence badges visible on all data points
- Corrections appended to original content, never silently edited
- No speculation presented as fact
- Headlines never use "BREAKING" or exclamation marks

### Pillar 2: AI Job Board & Aggregation

**Purpose**: The primary action surface for Persona B and the eventual destination for Persona A. The job board is what makes JobAnxiety.ai a daily-use platform, not just a one-time read.

**Content Types**:

1. **Job Listings** (`/jobs/[slug]`)
   - Aggregated from ATS feeds, job boards, and direct scraping
   - Each listing shows: title, company, location, salary (when available), source board, date first seen, freshness indicator, social proof metrics
   - AI category badge (core / adjacent / augmented)
   - "Apply" button links to original source (we aggregate, not intermediate)
   - Trust signals: "Verified active [X hours ago]", source attribution

2. **Category Landing Pages** (`/jobs/category/[category]`)
   - One page per AI job category (ML Engineer, Prompt Engineer, AI Safety, etc.)
   - Format: category description + salary range data + demand trend chart + filtered job listings
   - SEO-optimized title: "ML Engineer Jobs — [count] Open Roles, $[min]–$[max]K | JobAnxiety.ai"
   - Updated via ISR every 15 minutes

3. **Trending Jobs Feature** (homepage section)
   - Algorithmically ranked by engagement (apply clicks, views, saves)
   - Social proof displayed: "142 people applied today"
   - "New" badge for jobs < 48 hours old
   - "Closing soon" indicator for jobs > 25 days old
   - Refreshed every 5 minutes via Redis cache

4. **Job Market Pulse** (`/trends`)
   - Dashboard showing: total active AI roles, weekly delta, category breakdown, salary trends, geographic distribution
   - Every number links to methodology
   - Downloadable as CSV
   - Embeddable charts for journalists

**Social Proof Strategy**:

Social proof is critical for two reasons — it tells displaced workers "this job is real and active" (reducing application fatigue), and it creates urgency that drives engagement. Implementation rules:

```
Display rules:
  "X people applied today"        → show when X ≥ 5
  "X people viewed this week"     → fallback when daily applies < 5
  "New"                           → jobs posted < 48 hours ago
  "Closing soon"                  → jobs first seen > 25 days ago
  "Trending"                      → top 10% by engagement score this week
  "Verified active X hours ago"   → always show last freshness check time

Never:
  - Fabricate or inflate numbers
  - Show "0 people applied" (show nothing instead)
  - Show social proof on jobs older than 45 days (they're likely dead)
```

**Homepage Job Aggregation Display**:

The homepage must immediately communicate "this is a real, active job board with thousands of opportunities." The aggregated job count is the headline social proof:

```
"12,847 AI roles are hiring right now"
  ↳ Updated 8 minutes ago · Across 847 companies · From 12 job boards

[Trending This Week]
  🔥 142 people applied → Senior ML Engineer — Anthropic — Remote — $220–280K
  🔥 89 people applied  → AI Safety Researcher — DeepMind — London — £150–200K
  🔥 203 people applied → Prompt Engineer — Stripe — SF/Remote — $180–240K
  → See all 3,214 jobs posted this week
```

**Publishing Cadence**:
- Job listings: continuous (scrapers run every 15 min–1 hour)
- Category pages: auto-generated, ISR-refreshed
- Trending recalculation: every 5 minutes
- Market pulse: ISR every 15 minutes, full snapshot weekly

### Pillar 3: Career Guidance & Transition

**Purpose**: The editorial heart of the platform. This is where Persona A converts from "I'm scared" to "I have a plan," and where Persona B gets actionable frameworks for their job search.

**Content Types**:

1. **"Will AI Replace [Role]?" Series** (`/career-notes/ai-impact-[role]`)
   - One page per major role affected by AI (accountant, paralegal, copywriter, customer service rep, data entry, graphic designer, translator, radiologist, financial analyst, recruiter, etc.)
   - Format: honest assessment with data + displacement ratio for that role + skills that transfer + adjacent AI roles that hire from this background + real job listings
   - Word count: 2,000–3,000 words
   - Tone: honest, not reassuring. "Here's what's actually happening, here's what you can do"
   - Update cycle: every 6 months or when major shift occurs
   - This series is the single most important SEO asset — it targets the highest-intent anxiety queries

2. **Career Transition Guides** (`/career-notes/[guide-slug]`)
   - Actionable, step-by-step guides for career pivots
   - Topics:
     - "After the Layoff: A Week-by-Week Action Plan"
     - "How to Pivot Into AI Without a CS Degree"
     - "The Skills Map: What You Know vs What AI Employers Want"
     - "Career Change at 30/40/50: An Honest Guide"
     - "Negotiating Severance: What Most People Don't Know"
     - "Explaining a Layoff in Interviews: Scripts That Work"
     - "Best AI Certifications That Actually Lead to Jobs (and Which Don't)"
     - "Building an AI Portfolio When You're Starting from Zero"
     - "Remote AI Jobs: How to Stand Out in a Global Applicant Pool"
     - "The WARN Act Explained: Your Rights When Your Company Lays Off"
   - Word count: 2,500–4,000 words
   - Must include: real data from the platform, specific job listings, and at least one actionable template or framework
   - Tone: empathetic but practical ("I know this is hard. Here's what to do today.")

3. **Market Commentary** (`/career-notes/market/[slug]`)
   - Economist-led analysis pieces
   - Topics: monthly market briefs, quarterly deep dives, responses to major events
   - Word count: 1,500–2,500 words
   - Always data-first, opinion-second
   - Must distinguish between what the data shows and what it might mean

4. **First-Person Essays** (`/career-notes/stories/[slug]`)
   - Submitted or solicited stories from real people who've navigated AI-era career transitions
   - Not advice pieces — empathy pieces
   - Format: 800–1,500 words, first person, lightly edited for clarity
   - Must include a brief author bio and current role
   - Published with permission only, real names optional
   - Purpose: Persona A and B need to see themselves in someone else's story

**Publishing Cadence**:
- "Will AI Replace" series: 2 new roles per month, existing roles updated quarterly
- Career transition guides: 2 new guides per month
- Market commentary: weekly brief + monthly deep dive + quarterly report
- First-person essays: 2 per month (solicited)

### Pillar 4: Data & Methodology Transparency

**Purpose**: This pillar exists entirely for Persona C (journalists/researchers) and for the platform's long-term credibility. Every number on the site must be traceable, and this pillar is where the receipts live.

**Content Types**:

1. **Methodology Page** (`/methodology`)
   - The single most important trust page on the site
   - Contents: how jobs are classified, how layoffs are verified, confidence rating definitions, data sources listed, scraper frequency, AI classification model details, methodology version history
   - Updated every time the methodology changes (with changelog)
   - Word count: 2,000–3,000 words (comprehensive but scannable)
   - Must include: classification taxonomy, sample size, accuracy metrics, known limitations

2. **Frozen Snapshots** (`/trends/snapshot/[date]`)
   - Monthly data snapshots that never change after publication
   - Contents: all aggregate stats, methodology version used, downloadable CSV/JSON
   - Pre-formatted citation provided on every snapshot page
   - These are what journalists cite — they need permalink stability

3. **API Documentation** (`/api`)
   - Developer-friendly docs for the public API
   - Includes: endpoint reference, authentication, rate limits, response schemas, example queries
   - Must include a "For Journalists" section with simplified access instructions

4. **Press Page** (`/press`)
   - Pre-formatted data citations (copy-paste)
   - Embeddable chart widgets (iframe code)
   - Monthly data briefs (downloadable PDF)
   - Press contact with response time commitment
   - Brand assets for attribution (logos, color specs)

5. **Corrections Log** (`/corrections`)
   - Public record of every factual correction made
   - Format: date, original claim, corrected claim, source, affected pages
   - This is unusual for a data platform but standard for credible newsrooms
   - Its existence alone signals editorial integrity

**Publishing Cadence**:
- Methodology: updated as needed, at least quarterly review
- Snapshots: monthly (locked on the 3rd business day)
- API docs: updated with every API change
- Press page: monthly data brief
- Corrections: as needed (ideally rarely)

## 5. On-Page SEO Standards

### Title Tag Formula

```
[Primary Keyword] — [Value Proposition] | JobAnxiety.ai

Examples:
  "AI Layoff Tracker — Source-Verified Data Updated Daily | JobAnxiety.ai"
  "Will AI Replace Accountants? An Honest Assessment | JobAnxiety.ai"
  "ML Engineer Jobs — 2,400 Open Roles, $150K–$300K | JobAnxiety.ai"
  "After the Layoff: A Week-by-Week Action Plan | JobAnxiety.ai"
```

Rules:
- Keep under 60 characters (Google truncates beyond this)
- Primary keyword in the first 30 characters
- Never use clickbait or ALL CAPS
- Include dynamic data (job counts, salary ranges) when applicable — these create rich snippets and signal freshness

### Meta Description Formula

```
[Acknowledge the concern] + [what you'll find] + [trust signal].

Examples:
  "Track AI-related layoffs with source-verified data, WARN Act filings,
  and confidence ratings. Updated daily across 847 companies. Free API
  for researchers." (155 chars)

  "Wondering if AI will replace accountants? Data-driven analysis of
  automation risk, transferable skills, and 340 open AI-adjacent roles
  for finance professionals." (158 chars)

  "Just laid off? Week-by-week action plan covering severance negotiation,
  benefits, job search strategy, and interview prep. Written by people
  who've been there." (156 chars)
```

Rules:
- Keep between 140–160 characters
- Include a number or data point when possible (increases CTR)
- Match search intent: informational queries get informational descriptions, transactional queries mention action
- Never duplicate across pages

### Heading Structure

Every page follows a semantic heading hierarchy:

```html
<h1> — One per page. Contains the primary keyword.
       "AI Layoff Tracker: Source-Verified Data"

<h2> — Major sections. Contains secondary keywords.
       "Layoff Events This Month"
       "AI-Attributed Layoffs"
       "How We Verify Our Data"

<h3> — Subsections. Contains long-tail keywords.
       "What Does 'AI-Attributed' Mean?"
       "WARN Act Filings by State"

<h4> — Supporting detail. Used sparingly.
```

Rules:
- Never skip heading levels (no H1 → H3)
- H1 should be different from the title tag (related but not identical — covers more keyword surface)
- Headings should be scannable — a reader should understand the page by reading only the headings

### Internal Linking Rules

```
Every job listing      → company profile + relevant career guide
Every layoff event     → company profile + methodology + affected industry analysis
Every career guide     → related job listings + trends dashboard + other guides
Every snapshot         → methodology + API docs + press page
Homepage               → everything (hub-and-spoke center)
Anchor text            → descriptive, keyword-rich, never "click here"

Orphan page rule: Every page must be reachable within 3 clicks of the homepage.
New content rule: Every new page must be linked from at least 2 existing pages within 24 hours of publication.
```

### Structured Data (JSON-LD)

```json
// Job listings → JobPosting schema (enables Google Jobs integration)

  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "JobPosting",
  "title": "Senior ML Engineer",
```

## 7. Engagement & Retention Content Tactics

### Newsletter Strategy

**"The Market Brief"** — weekly email every Monday at 8am ET

Format:
```
Subject: [# AI jobs this week] open roles · [# layoff events] this month · The Market Brief

1. THE NUMBER: One headline stat with context (50 words)
2. WHAT HAPPENED: Top 3–5 layoff events or hiring surges, bulleted (100 words)
3. THE CHART: One embedded chart from the trends dashboard
4. ONE GUIDE: Link to the newest career guide or role analysis
5. THE BOARD: Top 5 trending jobs with social proof ("89 people applied")
6. ONE STORY: Link to a first-person essay
```

Segments:
- "Weekly Digest" — everyone (default)
- "Layoff Alerts" — immediate notification when a major event (>500) is confirmed
- "Job Alerts" — daily digest of new listings matching saved filters

### Returning User Hooks

Content features that drive weekly return visits:

1. **"Your Industry" Dashboard**: Persona A bookmarks this. Shows AI impact metrics filtered to their industry, updated weekly.
2. **Trending Jobs Board**: Persona B checks this daily. Social proof numbers change, new listings appear.
3. **Market Pulse Widget**: Homepage stat bar. 15-minute ISR refresh means it's always "new" enough to check.
4. **"This Week in AI Jobs" Banner**: Changes weekly, highlights the single most important trend.
5. **Saved Searches + Alerts**: Persona B sets up job filters and gets daily digests.
